The History of Vinyls and Physical Media

How It All Started

Way back before Bluetooth speakers and Spotify playlists, people were inventing ways to actually trap sound. Shoutout to Thomas Edison and Emile Berliner for basically making it possible to vibe to music without a live band in your living room.

Vinyl’s Golden Age

By the 1950s, vinyl wasn’t just a thing — it was *the* thing. LPs gave artists the power to tell full stories, and 45s were spinning in every diner and dance hall. It wasn’t just music — it was a whole vibe you could hold in your hands.

Enter: The Digital Takeover

Fast forward to the 80s, and CDs pulled up like the new kid at school. Smaller, shinier, digital — the future, apparently. Then came MP3s, then streaming, and suddenly music went from something you *kept* to something you just *clicked.*

The Vinyl Comeback

And now? People realized you can’t beat the ritual — flipping a record, hearing the crackle, feeling *something* real. Vinyl’s back, and honestly, she’s thriving.
